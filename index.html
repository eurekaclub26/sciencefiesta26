Choose Your Path

Two Tracks.
One Goal.

Every team must choose one track before submitting their proposal. The two tracks are judged and awarded separately — meaning 1st, 2nd, and 3rd place will be selected independently within each track. Choose the track that best suits your team's strengths.
Research Track

The Deep Blue

For teams who want to go deep into research or engineering.

  • A working model or research paper is required
  • Rigorous, evidence-based, and thorough
  • Focus on scientific methodology and experimentation
  • Judged independently from The White Cap
Pitch Track

The White Cap

For teams who want to think like entrepreneurs.

  • Working model is optional — not required
  • A business pitch is the primary deliverable
  • Ideas should be viable, creative, and commercially thought through
  • Judged independently from The Deep Blue

Safety Protocols

All teams must follow these rules without exception. Any project found in violation may be disqualified from the exhibition.

1 · General Laboratory Conduct

📋Follow all standard laboratory safety rules as taught in VIHS Science practicals.
👁️Any experimentation involving chemical reactions or heat must be conducted under teacher or qualified adult supervision.
🗑️Safely dispose of all materials. No chemicals or biological waste in standard bins or sinks without prior approval.

2 · Chemical & Fire Safety

🚫Firearms, weapons, and highly hazardous substances are strictly forbidden.
🔥No direct flames, explosives, or highly flammable materials within the exhibition area.
🌱Prioritise Green Chemistry — use non-toxic, household alternatives wherever possible.

3 · Electrical & Mechanical Safety

⚡All electrical projects must meet standard safety requirements — no short circuits or shock risks.
🔌No exposed live wires. All connections must be insulated with electrical tape or heat-shrink tubing.
🔋High-capacity batteries (e.g. LiPo) must be stored in fire-retardant bags and handled with extreme care.

4 · Biological & Ethics Standards

🌿Health and physical integrity of any living organism (plants or small invertebrates) must be maintained at all times.
🦠Use of microbes (bacteria, fungi, etc.) and radiation is strictly prohibited per school regulations.
🔭Studies involving restricted areas must be conducted via simulations or research papers only.

5 · Exhibition Hall Safety

🔊Lights, sounds, and colours must be reasonable and non-distracting — no high-frequency strobes.
🏗️Display boards must be self-standing and sturdy enough to prevent falling on visitors.
